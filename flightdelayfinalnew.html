Flight Delay & Cancellations Analysis¶

*Katherine Li¶

Introduction¶

This project investigates on questions related to flight delays and flight cancellations, and it aims to share some insights for relevant entities on how to avoid delays and optimize the operation of relevant facilities. The main topics include: in which month(s) do flight delays occur most frequently? Do fly delays happen more during the daytime or the nighttime? Is the frequency of flight delays correlated to the ariline carriers in any way?

Plus, scroll down to the end to find some visualization depicting and showcasing more in-depth analysis!

The U.S. Department of Transportation's (DOT) Bureau of Transportation Statistics tracks the on-time performance of domestic flights operated by large air carriers. Summary information on the number of on-time, delayed, canceled, and diverted flights is published in DOT's monthly Air Travel Consumer Report and in this dataset of 2015 flight delays and cancellations.

Notes before Getting Started¶

For this relatively large dataset, explanations to better utilize the data for analysis are as follows.

  1. For day of the week, 1 means Monday, while 7 means Sunday - this could be verified by checking what day is it for 2015/1/1 on the calendar, and it is an Thursday, noted as "4" in the dataset.
  2. For delays, there are departure delays and arrival delays. Consider these two situations separately.
  3. In terms of earlier in the day versus later in the day, use 1200 as the cut-off point, as in 12pm (noon) in real life context. When it is equal to 1200, consider it as the later of the day, as 0000 (12am) is taken in as earlier of the day.
  4. To determine whether the flight is in earlier or later of the day, check the scheduled departure and scheduled arrival time accordingly.
  5. For the cancelled flights, there are only scheduled departure time recorded in the dataset.
  6. When it comes to taxi times, consider taxi out for origin airports, and taxi in for destination airports.

Topic 1¶

Do flight delays happen more often later in the day compared to earlier in the day?¶

From the results, we could conclude that both departure delays and arrival delays happened more often later in the day in 2015. Arrival delays are more pronounced in this pattern - for arrival delays, those happened later in the day takes up a bigger proportion of total arrival delays. For departure delays, those happened later in the day takes up a smaller proportion of total departure delays, relatively.

This result kind of makes sense as airline companies normally line up their flights by available air crafts. If one flight is delayed earlier in the day, the situation would accumulate and affect the next flight that is supposed to be flew with the same air craft.

Topic 2¶

Does the response to Topic 1 depend on the month of the year?¶

No, the response to Topic 1 does not depend on the month of the year - as in for each month in 2015, both departure delays and arrival delays happened more often later in the day.

For departure delays, looking at the proportion that the late delays take up out of the total delays, May had this phenomenon most pronounced in 2015; December had this phenomenon least pronounced in 2015.

For arrival delays, looking at the proportion that the late delays take up out of the total delays, September had this phenomenon most pronounced in 2015; and once again, December this phenomenon least pronounced in 2015.

Topic 3¶

Does the response to Topic 1 depend on the airline? Which airlines have this phenomenon more pronounced and less pronounced?¶

The resposne to Topic 1 does not depend on the airline - as for all the airlines included, both departure delays and arrival delays happened more often later in the day than earlier in the day.

For departure delays, looking at the proportion that the late delays take up out of the total delays, airline B6, as in Jetblue Airways had this phenomenon most pronounced in 2015; airline UA, as in United Airlines had this phenomenon least pronounced in 2015.

For arrival delays, looking at the proportion that the late delays take up out of the total delays, airline WN, as in Southwest Airlines had this phenomenon most pronounced in 2015; airline NK, as in Spirit Airlines had this phenomenon least pronounced in 2015.
